# Quality Manual

**Document reference:** QM-001
**Version:** 4.2
**Date:** September 2024
**Owner:** Quality Manager
**Next review:** September 2026

---

## 1. Introduction

Brookmere Manufacturing Ltd ("Brookmere" or "the Company") is committed to quality in everything we do. This manual describes how the Company manages quality across our operations.

This manual is broadly aligned with the principles of ISO 9001:2008.

---

## 2. Scope

This Quality Manual applies to the design, manufacture, and supply of metal components from our facility in Sheffield, UK. It applies to all permanent and temporary employees involved in production.

---

## 3. Quality policy

Brookmere is committed to delivering quality products to our customers. The Managing Director is fully behind our quality programme.

The full quality policy statement is displayed on the noticeboard in reception.

---

## 4. Responsibilities

The Quality Manager is responsible for maintaining the Quality Management System. Production staff are responsible for following their training and producing quality work.

---

## 5. Quality goals

Brookmere aims to:

- Reduce customer complaints
- Improve on-time delivery
- Maintain high standards

We review these aims from time to time.

---

## 6. Resources

Brookmere provides the equipment, tools, and training necessary for staff to do their jobs. The Operations Director allocates resources as appropriate.

---

## 7. Training

New starters receive an induction. Further training is provided as needed when new equipment is introduced or when issues arise.

---

## 8. Production

All production work is carried out according to our standard operating procedures, which are kept on the shop floor. Supervisors check work before it is shipped to customers.

If a problem is found during production, it should be reported to the supervisor.

---

## 9. Customer feedback

Customer complaints are logged by the sales team and passed to the Quality Manager for investigation. Most complaints are responded to within a reasonable timeframe.

---

## 10. Document control

Procedures and work instructions are kept on the shared drive. Old versions are kept in the archive folder for reference.

---

## 11. Preventive and corrective action

When something goes wrong, the Quality Manager investigates and takes preventive action to stop it happening again. This may involve updating procedures or providing additional training.

---

## 12. Continual improvement

Brookmere is committed to continual improvement. Suggestions from staff are welcomed via the suggestion box in the canteen.

---

## 13. Manual review

This manual is reviewed every two years, or sooner if the Quality Manager considers it necessary.
