Lab overview

You'll use the no-code Agent Builder in watsonx Orchestrate to build a four-agent compliance pipeline. Upload a quality manual through Chat with Documents and receive a structured gap analysis, a rewritten manual and an executive compliance report.

Meet Olivia

Profile

Quality manager at a mid-size manufacturer. Six weeks out from a stage-1 ISO 9001 audit. She inherited a stack of legacy quality manuals — most predate ISO 9001:2015. Experienced with QMS principles but stretched for time, and the audit clock is ticking.

Goals

  • Bring every legacy manual in line with ISO 9001:2015 without spending weeks per document.
  • Pass the stage-1 audit at the first attempt.
  • Move from manually re-reading every manual to a structured triage-and-rewrite workflow.

Needs

  • A consistent gap analysis against all 28 clauses, with status, finding and recommendation.
  • Rewrites that keep her existing manual structure so staff don't have to re-train.
  • An executive-ready report she can hand to senior management before the audit.

Concerns

  • Trusting an AI to interpret nuanced standard requirements correctly.
  • Hallucinations that introduce non-compliant or invented wording.
  • An output she can't edit or pull apart — she needs to keep editorial control.

Architecture — four agents

Strict sequential pipeline. The orchestrator owns the chat; each collaborator does one job and hands back.

Orchestratorcompliance-orchestrator — ReAct, 3 collaborators, Chat with Documents enabled
Collaborator 1iso9001-gap-analyser — Default, 28 clauses embedded, no tools
Collaborator 2policy-rewriter — ReAct, web_search tool
Collaborator 3compliance-reporter — Default, no tools

ISO 9001:2015 clauses assessed (28 total)

Context (4) + Leadership (5)
  • 4.1 Organisation and its context
  • 4.2 Interested parties
  • 4.3 Scope of the QMS
  • 4.4 QMS and its processes
  • 5.1 Leadership and commitment
  • 5.2 Quality policy
  • 5.3 Roles, responsibilities, authorities
Planning (6) + Support (7)
  • 6.1 Risks and opportunities
  • 6.2 Quality objectives and planning
  • 6.3 Planning of changes
  • 7.1 Resources
  • 7.2 Competence
  • 7.3 Awareness
  • 7.4 Communication
  • 7.5 Documented information
Operation (8)
  • 8.1 Operational planning and control
  • 8.2 Requirements for products/services
  • 8.3 Design and development
  • 8.4 Externally provided processes
  • 8.5 Production and service provision
  • 8.6 Release of products and services
  • 8.7 Control of nonconforming outputs
Performance (9) + Improvement (10)
  • 9.1 Monitoring, measurement, analysis
  • 9.2 Internal audit
  • 9.3 Management review
  • 10.1 Improvement — general
  • 10.2 Nonconformity and corrective action
  • 10.3 Continual improvement
